Sprint Review Suggestions

Recommendations

It seems like Sprint Review meetings are not very effective for your team; thus, we recommend that you skip them not to waste time unless you can make them become effective given the information below. Here are more details on how to use Sprint Planning for your team.

  • Here is why your Sprint Reviews might be ineffective (Arman Kamran).
    • Not holding any Sprint Reviews
    • Turning Sprint Reviews into Reporting Sessions
    • Sleep Therapy Session
    • Surprise Party
    • Ghost “Dev” Town
    • Only Works on Developer’s Machine
    • Missing Stakeholders
    • Sprint Gating (Scrum Fall)
    • Silent Treatment
    • The All-Knowing-PO
  • Here are fixes to the most common mistakes team’s make in Sprint Reviews (Michael Küsters)
    • Activity Reporting. Quick fix: Forget about YOU. Talk about THEM (the customer!) and their needs in terms of the Product!
    • Ticket Management. Quick fix: Forbid the use of ticket information during the Review.
    • Powerpoint. Quick fix: Forbid the use of Presentation software during the Review. Do a DEMO
    • Disconnect. Quick fix: Make user statements in the Review lead to real change in process and product.
    • No Action. Quick fix: Hand the product to the user and see what happens.
    • Filling the timebox. Quick fix: Focus on essentials, keep the Review as short as possible.
  • “Some of our Sprint Meetings are so simple and it seems to be a waste of time spending a whole day just for planning and reviewing. I think it needs to be adjusted based on the complexity of the project that we are working on.”
