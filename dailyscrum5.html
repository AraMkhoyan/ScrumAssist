Daily Scrum Suggestions

Recommendations

We recommend that you skip Daily Scrum Meetings. This is mainly because of your current team behavior of not using daily scrum yet being productive. Here are more details on how to use Daily Scrum for your team.

  • If you don’t hold Daily Scrum meetings - Run it on emergency situations (Prokopets, n.d.)
  • Run if people are confused about what to work on for today and make sure that during the meeting you follow the structure and everyone shares their progress and updates (Kniberg, 2015)
  • If you hold Daily Scrum meetings - You can skip once in a week or very rarely (Prokopets, n.d.)
  • You should skip it because…
    • If people are abusing the meeting time (Mersino, 2019)
    • If you care a lot about performance and prefer your employees to work (Mersino, 2019)
    • If the team is high-performing (Lai, n.d.)
    • If a Scrum team has been together for several months, or even years, they may be tempted to do away with the Daily Scrum. One argument you may hear is “we already talk all day long, so we don’t need this meeting”.
    • If the team is collocated and highly collaborative (Lai, n.d., Bindlish, 2016)
    • If a team sits right next to each other and collaborates very effectively, the team members likely talk to one another frequently. In this case, the Daily Scrum may be perceived as an administrative event that adds little value.
    • If the team is highly effective at swarming (Lai, n.d.)
    • If a team consistently swarms on backlog items, meaning that they work together on User Stories and/or practice Pair Programming, the Daily Scrum may be seen as unnecessary by the team.
    • If not planned to be in the morning (Martín de Agar, J., 2018)
    • If people are usually being late (Martín de Agar, J., 2018)
    • Scrum Master or Product Owner is not usually present (Martín de Agar, J., 2018)
  • If you have the meetings and yet notice it is not efficient, this might be due to…
    • Teammates are not being collaborative during the meeting and the meeting does not serve its purpose
    • The 3 key questions are not being addressed
    • The Product Owner skipped the meeting
    • There is no fixed structure in place for the meetings (e.g., fixed time)
