Daily Scrum Suggestions

Recommendations

We suggest that you hold Daily Scrum meetings for 15 minutes every other day, mainly due to your small team size. Here is more information about Daily Scrum and how you should utilize it.

  • Should be used for group 4-6 - 15 mins every second day (Diebold, Ostberg, Wagner, & Zendler, 2015)
  • Sometimes when people are confused about what to work on for that day, you can have a Daily Scrum even if it was not scheduled (Kniberg, 2015)
  • If you think that the team is well aware of their daily tasks and there is no need for quick collaboration, Daily Scrum can be skipped (Prokpets, n.d.)
  • If a Scrum team has been together for several months, or even years, they may be tempted to do away with the Daily Scrum. One argument you may hear is “we already talk all day long, so we don’t need this meeting” (Lai, n.d.).
  • If a team sits right next to each other and collaborates very effectively, the team members likely talk to one another frequently. In this case, the Daily Scrum may be perceived as an administrative event that adds little value (Lai, n.d., Bindlish, 2016).
  • Keep in mind that Daily Scrum is not effective for two teams (not revealed in the study) because… (Mortada, Ayas, & Hebig, 2020)
    • Not all key questions according to Scrum Guide are addressed
    • Not all team members contribute
    • Daily scrum events take longer than 15 minutes
    • No fixed time for the daily scrum event
  • Also, here are some recommendations according to Scrum Guide to keep in mind while having your Daily Scrum Meetings
    • The Scrum Master should allocate at least 2 minutes to each developer to answer to 3 main questions highlighted on the Scrum Guide
    • The Daily Scrum should be the first thing every morning the developer team does, as their work during the day will depend on what was discussed in the Daily Scrum meeting
